## Creating the PowerPoint/Google Slides Presentation

### **Option 1: Google Slides (Recommended - Easiest)**

1. **Open the content file:**
   - Location: `/app/LessonPlanAI_Presentation_Content.md`
   - Download or copy the content

2. **Create new Google Slides:**
   - Go to slides.google.com
   - Click "Blank presentation"
   - Choose a professional template (or use blank)

3. **Copy slide by slide:**
   - Each slide is clearly marked with "## SLIDE X"
   - Copy the title
   - Copy the content
   - Follow formatting hints (colors, icons, bullets)

4. **Design tips:**
   - Use consistent color scheme (blue/purple gradient suggested)
   - Add icons from Google Slides icon library
   - Keep text minimal, use bullet points
   - Add your school/company logo on every slide

5. **Add screenshots:**
   - Log into your LessonPlan AI demo
   - Take screenshots of key features:
     * Lesson plan creation form
     * Generated lesson plan output
     * At-risk students list
     * Admin review queue
     * Analytics dashboard
   - Insert into slides where marked [Screenshot]

6. **Download as PowerPoint:**

   - Now you can email it or present from PowerPoint

## Quick Tips for Success

**For the Presentation:**
1. Practice timing - aim for 15 minutes
2. Have demo site open in another tab
3. Show actual at-risk student list (most impactful)
4. Focus on their specific pain points
5. End with clear call-to-action (schedule trial)

**For the One-Pager:**
1. Print on high-quality paper if meeting in person
2. Send as PDF via email for virtual meetings
3. Use as leave-behind after presentation
4. Include in follow-up emails

**Follow-Up Strategy:**
- Day 1: Send materials
- Day 3: Follow-up email if no response
- Day 5: Phone call
- Day 7: Final email with deadline reminder
